Introduction

Welcome to DataMate, your private, browser-based data analysis tool. DataMate allows you to quickly inspect, filter, and analyze CSV and Excel files without uploading your sensitive data to any server.

Key Benefits

100% Private: Files are processed locally in your browser. No data leaves your computer.
Instant Analysis: Load files and get results immediately without complex setups.
Versatile Support: Supports both standard CSV files and modern Excel (.xlsx) spreadsheets.

Getting Started

1. Uploading Data

To begin, simply drag and drop your file into the "Upload" box on the left panel, or click it to browse your computer.

Don't have a file handy? Click the Example Data button in the top right to load a sample dataset.

2. Viewing Data

Once loaded, your data appears in the main grid on the right. The table supports:

  • Sticky Headers: Keep column names visible while scrolling.
  • Virtual Scrolling: Handles large lists smoothly (display limited to top 100 rows for performance by default).

Analysis Tools

Filtering

  1. Select a column from the dropdown in the Filter section.
  2. Type the text or number you're looking for.
  3. Click Add Filter.

You can add multiple filters to narrow down your results. Active filters appear as chips above the list.

Sorting

Organize your data by selecting a column in the Sort section and clicking the sort button. Click again to toggle between Ascending and Descending order.

metrics

Get quick statistics on your numeric columns:

  • Sum: Total of all values.
  • Average: The mean value.
  • Min/Max: The smallest and largest values.
  • Count: The number of non-empty rows.

Select the column and operation, then click Compute to see the result.

Exporting

Found what you need? Click the Export button in the top header to download your currently filtered view as a new CSV file.

Troubleshooting

My file won't load?

Ensure your file is a valid .csv or .xlsx file. Very large files (>50MB) might be slow depending on your browser's memory limits.

Dates are not sorting correctly?

Currently, sorting works best on standard text and numbers. Complex date formats might sort alphabetically.
